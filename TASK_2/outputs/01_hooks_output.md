# Output 01 — 8 Scroll-Stopping Hooks
**Brand:** GlowLab Skincare | **Product:** Vitamin C Brightening Serum
**Tool Used:** Claude (claude.ai) | **Prompt:** prompts/01_hooks_prompt.md

---

## Hook 1 — CURIOSITY
*"I wasn't expecting this serum to actually work... but here's my skin after 14 days."*

> **Why it works:** Opens a loop. Viewer needs to see the result to close it.

---

## Hook 2 — PAIN POINT
*"If your skin looks dull no matter how much water you drink or sleep you get — keep watching."*

> **Why it works:** Directly mirrors the viewer's frustration. They feel seen immediately.

---

## Hook 3 — RESULT
*"My dark spots are literally fading and I've only been using this for two weeks."*

> **Why it works:** Leads with the outcome. Aspirational viewers stop to find out how.

---

## Hook 4 — CONTROVERSY
*"I wasted ₹8,000 on branded serums before I found one that actually did something."*

> **Why it works:** Challenges the assumption that expensive = effective. Creates instant relatability.

---

## Hook 5 — RELATABILITY
*"Okay bestie, I need to tell you about the one thing that finally fixed my dull skin."*

> **Why it works:** "Bestie" framing = peer-to-peer trust, not brand-to-consumer selling.

---

## Hook 6 — QUESTION
*"Be honest — when was the last time your skin actually glowed without a filter?"*

> **Why it works:** Asks the viewer something personal. Self-reflection = engagement.

---

## Hook 7 — STORY
*"Six months ago I stopped wearing foundation to work because I was embarrassed about my skin. This is what changed."*

> **Why it works:** Opens a compelling personal story. Viewer stays to hear the resolution.

---

## Hook 8 — SOCIAL PROOF
*"92% of women who used this saw brighter skin in 14 days — I was one of them."*

> **Why it works:** Specific numbers (92%, 14 days) feel like real data, not marketing fluff.

---
