Prompt 01 Output

8 Scroll-Stopping Hooks

Different angles — same goal: stop the viewer in the first 3 seconds

Curiosity
"I wasn't expecting this serum to actually work... but here's my skin after 14 days."
Opens a loop. Viewer needs to see the result to close it.
Pain Point
"If your skin looks dull no matter how much water you drink or sleep you get — keep watching."
Directly mirrors the viewer's frustration. They feel seen immediately.
Result
"My dark spots are literally fading and I've only been using this for two weeks."
Leads with the outcome. Aspirational viewers stop to find out how.
Controversy
"I wasted ₹8,000 on branded serums before I found one that actually did something."
Challenges the assumption that expensive = effective. Instant relatability.
Relatability
"Okay bestie, I need to tell you about the one thing that finally fixed my dull skin."
"Bestie" framing = peer-to-peer trust, not brand-to-consumer selling.
Question
"Be honest — when was the last time your skin actually glowed without a filter?"
Asks the viewer something personal. Self-reflection drives engagement.
Story
"Six months ago I stopped wearing foundation to work because of my skin. This is what changed."
Opens a compelling personal loop that the viewer needs to close.
Social Proof
"92% of women who used this saw brighter skin in 14 days — I was one of them."
Specific numbers feel like real data, not marketing claims.
